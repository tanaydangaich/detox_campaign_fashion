# Greenpeace Toxic Waste Campaign Targets

A comprehensive database of public companies targeted by Greenpeace for toxic waste pollution, including air, land, water, and nuclear contamination.

## Overview

This repository documents public companies that have been specifically targeted by Greenpeace campaigns for various types of toxic waste pollution. The data is compiled from official Greenpeace reports, investigations, and campaign documentation.

- Coal & Energy

## Categories of Toxic Waste

### Water Pollution
- **Fashion Industry**: Hazardous chemicals, PFCs, alkylphenols, hormone disruptors in textile production
- **Chemical Industry**: PFAS "forever chemicals" contaminating drinking water
- **Oil & Gas**: Fracking waste, hydrocarbon chemicals, volatile organic compounds

### Air Pollution
- **Coal Power**: Sulfur dioxide (SO2), nitrogen oxides (NOx), particulate matter (PM), mercury
- **Chemical Industry**: PFAS air emissions, toxic chemical releases

### Land Pollution
- **Electronics**: E-waste, PVC, brominated flame retardants (BFRs)
- **Plastics**: Single-use plastic packaging, microplastics

### Nuclear Waste
- **Nuclear Industry**: Radioactive waste dumping, unsafe storage in Siberia, ocean dumping

## Key Campaigns

### Detox My Fashion (2011-2020)
Challenged major fashion brands to eliminate hazardous chemicals from textile production. 80 companies representing 15% of global clothing production committed to phase out toxic chemicals by 2020.

**Major participants**: H&M, Zara, Nike, Adidas, Puma, Primark

### PFAS "Forever Chemicals"
Targeted chemical manufacturers for PFAS contamination of drinking water and concealing health risks since the 1960s-1970s.

**Companies**: 3M, DuPont, Chemours, Dow Chemical

**Legal Impact**: $18+ billion in settlements

### Patagonia Fracking Investigation
Exposed illegal toxic waste dumps from fracking operations in Patagonia's Vaca Muerta region.

**Companies**: Shell, TotalEnergies, ExxonMobil, BP (Pan American Energy)

### Guide to Greener Electronics
Ranks electronics manufacturers on toxic chemicals, e-waste, and sustainability.

**Major targets**: Samsung (failing), Amazon (failing), Apple, Microsoft, Dell, HP, Lenovo, LG

### European Coal Health Impact
Documented health costs and premature deaths from coal power plant pollution in Europe.

**Companies**: RWE (Germany), EPH (Czech Republic)

**Impact**: 7,600+ premature deaths annually, €48/MWh health costs

## Key Findings

### Fashion Industry
- Nearly 75% of Detox Campaign participants eliminated dangerous PFCs
- Represents "major paradigm shift in the clothing industry" - Greenpeace CEO

### Chemical Industry
- 3M and DuPont knew of PFAS health hazards since the 1960s-70s but concealed evidence
- UN human rights experts condemned DuPont and Chemours for PFAS contamination
- Companies exploit regulatory loopholes to avoid reporting PFAS releases

### Oil & Gas
- Shell, Total, ExxonMobil, BP dumped toxic fracking waste in illegal open ponds
- Contamination threatened agricultural fields and drinking water within 5km

### Electronics
- Electronic waste reached 65 million tonnes globally
- Samsung, Amazon, Huawei received failing grades for toxic chemicals and sustainability
- Few companies followed through on commitments to phase out PVC and BFRs

### Coal Power
- Top 10 European coal companies caused 7,600 premature deaths in 2016
- RWE generates €48 in health costs per MWh of electricity produced

## Legal and Financial Impact

- **PFAS Settlements**: $18 billion total ($12.5B from 3M, $1.2B from DuPont)
- **UN Condemnations**: DuPont and Chemours
- **Regulatory Changes**: Multiple commitments to eliminate toxic chemicals by 2020
